Understanding Trauma-Informed Approaches

The CNWL Trauma-Informed Approaches mobile application represents a significant advancement in the delivery of mental health resources. Developed in collaboration with mental health professionals from Central and North West London NHS Foundation Trust, this application aligns with the NHS Long Term Plan (2019), which has established a ten-year roadmap for mental health services to become trauma-informed.

The fundamental shift in trauma-informed care moves away from asking "What is wrong with you?" to instead asking "What has happened to you?" This perspective recognizes that trauma experiences are common and require specialized approaches to care.

Trauma-informed approaches acknowledge the relationship between people's life experiences and the development of mental health difficulties. Research has shown that experiencing traumatic and/or adverse life events can place individuals at higher risk of a range of health and social difficulties (WHO, 2014).

The CNWL Trauma-Informed Approaches framework recognizes that in times of distress, people experiencing mental health difficulties often feel unstable, making forward progress challenging. Psychological stabilization—the process of learning and implementing various skills and strategies to manage distress—forms the foundation of the app's therapeutic approach.

Resource Library

Access to CNWL's extensive Stabilisation Manual, comprising ten evidence-based workbooks that introduce users to various stabilization skills.

🧠

Grounding Techniques

Quick-access exercises including the 5-4-3-2-1 technique, deep breathing, body scan, and object focus to help reconnect with the present moment.

⏱️

Meditation Timer

Customizable meditation timer with ambient sounds and progress tracking to develop a consistent mindfulness practice.

📝

Mood Journal

Secure mood journal for private tracking of emotional patterns and triggers, fostering self-awareness and emotional literacy.

📅

Implementation Intentions

Structured activity planning with calendar integration to support positive habit formation through when, where, and how planning.

💭

CBT Worksheets

Digital cognitive behavioral therapy exercises with save functionality to apply evidence-based techniques to challenge unhelpful thought patterns.
